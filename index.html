Comparison Table

Feature Cemented TKA Cementless TKA
Target Patient Older patients or those with poor bone quality Younger, active patients with excellent bone quality
Stability Immediate stability through bone cement Relies on biological fixation (longer recovery time)
Recovery Faster recovery due to immediate fixation Potential for initial instability
Longevity May deteriorate over time (cement wear or fracture) Potentially greater longevity with successful bone growth
Cost More affordable due to established methods Higher cost due to advanced materials and designs
Revision Surgery More complex due to cement removal Simpler revisions as no cement is involved
Suitability Best for low-activity individuals Best for active individuals with good bone quality
Infection Risk Reduced due to antibiotic-laden cement Comparable but without cement-based antibiotics
